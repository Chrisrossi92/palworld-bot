# Competitor Positioning

This document records positioning after discovering PalVerse, an existing Palworld Discord bot listed on Top.gg. The goal is not to clone PalVerse feature-for-feature. The goal is to sharpen the product around Discord community engagement and server-owner value.

## Market Signal

Competition validates the market.

PalVerse appears to be primarily a player-facing Palworld RPG and collection bot. That is useful proof that Discord communities want Palworld-themed game loops, but it should not pull this product into a feature race.

## Product Position

This product should not be positioned as merely another Palworld bot.

The stronger position is:

> Palworld community engagement platform for Discord server owners.

Player-facing features matter because players create activity. The durable business value, however, comes from helping Discord owners and operators keep communities active, configurable, measurable, and worth returning to.

## Differentiation

Prioritize owner/operator features:

- Server-owner dashboard.
- Engagement analytics.
- Configurable spawn and event tools.
- Server-specific campaigns.
- Branded and community-specific experiences.
- Retention tools.
- Guild health and activity reporting.
- Reusable multi-game SaaS platform foundations.

Player-facing features should support the owner-facing retention and engagement thesis. Paldeck, favorites, nicknames, achievements, battles, raids, profiles, and collection improvements still belong on the roadmap, but they should connect back to server activity, campaigns, social competition, or retention.

## What Not To Do

- Do not chase every competitor feature.
- Do not frame the roadmap as a PalVerse parity checklist.
- Do not over-invest in isolated solo RPG mechanics if they do not help a server become more active.
- Do not let player-facing polish displace the dashboard, analytics, and owner controls that create monetizable differentiation.

## Strategic Priority

The next product phase after Supabase stabilization should focus on:

1. Owner/dashboard foundation.
2. Discord OAuth.
3. Basic analytics.
4. Paldeck/profile improvements.

This order keeps player value moving while building the product surface that makes the bot a SaaS platform for community operators.
